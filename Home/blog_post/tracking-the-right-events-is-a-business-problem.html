Tracking the right events is a business problem

November 22, 2015November 22, 2015 admin 0 Comment Uncategorized

Events are anything that a user does inside your mobile application. It can be searching for a cab, subscribing to your newsletter, adding a product to the shopping cart or sharing your article in their social networking circles.

Events are mostly pre-defined meaning after an app is launched, very few additions or deletions happen in terms of events unless a major revamp is taking place. This makes tracking events – a supposedly easy task. You have almost everything pre-defined, very few alterations and yet very few clients actually make use of tracking events.






There is one major reason we think that event tracking has been under-utilized and that is this thinking that tracking events and their properties is essentially a programmer’s task. We meet countless clients who delegate the task of defining events and event properties to their mobile developers with the assumption that it is technical and they can always make corrections later. I am here to explain how we are missing out on a vital metric that could help us understand our clients because of our recklessness.

Events and event properties signify what is getting clicked inside your mobile application and the values that your users are providing. For example, if there is search functionality inside your app, would you not want to know what is the most searched item or query? Would you not want to know if there is a particular brand which gets added to cart but is never purchased? Would you like to send a push notification to users who have purchased a particular product in the last month? We cannot do any of these things without the proper tracking of events and their properties?



We @ rocQ understand how vital these metrics are in order to evaluate how your users are using your mobile application. We recommend taking event tracking seriously to our clients and would make the same appeal to all of you using app analytics across the world to do the same.
